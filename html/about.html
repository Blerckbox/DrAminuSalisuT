About

Dr Aminu Salisu Tsauri, PhD

Dr. Aminu Salisu Tsauri, Ph.D, FIICA, was born in Tsauri town, Kurfi Local Government Area, within Katsina Central Senatorial District. His early foundation was built upon Qur’anic and Islamic education, which shaped his moral discipline and guided his lifelong commitment to service and integrity. He attended Tsauri Model Primary School in 1993, Government Rural Boarding Secondary School, Safana in 1996, and later Government Secondary School, Malumfashi in 1999.

He obtained his NCE in Biology and Geography from Isa Kaita College of Education, Dutsinma in 2006; his B.Sc. (Ed.) in Science Education (Geography) from Umaru Musa Yar’adua University, Katsina in 2011; a Certificate in Computer Studies from the Federal College of Education, Katsina; and a Master’s Degree in Science Education from Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria in 2015. From 2017 to 2019, he successfully completed his Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Science Education at Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria—within two years—with a dissertation titled Impact of Computer Animation, Virtual and Blended Models on Academic Performance, Creativity, Interest, and Retention in Cartographic Techniques among Undergraduate Geography Students in Northwestern Nigeria.

In 2020, he attended a short training course in Research and Grant Management at the University of Cambridge, United Kingdom, which expanded his exposure to international best practices in academic research and innovation management.

He began his teaching career in primary and secondary schools across Kurfi, Dutsinma, and Katsina Local Government Areas, where he taught English, Mathematics, Basic Science and Technology, Physical and Health Education, Biology, and Geography. At the Umaru Musa Yar’adua University, Katsina, he served as a Lecturer in the Department of Science and Vocational Education, Faculty of Education, where he taught both undergraduate and postgraduate courses including Philosophy of Science Education, Research Methods and Statistics, Science Teaching Methods, Curriculum Studies, Environmental Management, Quantitative Techniques, and Measurement and Evaluation. He also served as Level Adviser, Postgraduate Coordinator, and Pioneer Deputy Director, School of Continuing Education.

He supervised over one hundred undergraduate projects and more than fifty postgraduate dissertations, published over forty scholarly articles in reputable journals, and participated in over fifty academic conferences locally and internationally.

In the realm of political service, He remained a dedicated and loyal member of the All Progressives Congress (APC), contributing actively to the success of the present administration. He served as Director General, Katsinawan Dikko Ga Dikko Movement; Secretary, Directorate for Youth Mobilisation, Tinubu/Dikko Campaign Council, Katsina State; Chairman, Inaugural Lecture Committee for the Swearing-in of the Current Administration; and Member, Publicity Committee for the Governor and Deputy’s Inauguration Ceremony.

Currently, he serves as the Executive Secretary, Katsina State Scholarship Board, established in 1976 under the Katsina State Scholarship Board Law, Cap 123, Laws of Katsina State, 1991 (as amended). Under his leadership, the Board has undergone remarkable transformation aligned with His Excellency’s “Building Your Future Agenda,” focusing on digitalization, transparency, inclusiveness, and merit-based scholarship administration.

The Board has disbursed over ₦1.9 billion to indigent Katsina students studying within Nigeria; sponsored over one hundred students abroad in fields such as Medicine, Artificial Intelligence, and Bioengineering; introduced Academic Excellence and Inclusiveness Scholarships; supported students with disabilities and those affected by insecurity; and initiated the Katsina State Scholarship Trust Fund to ensure sustainable scholarship financing.

Beyond academia and politics, He is passionate about community empowerment and education reform. He served as Chairman, Tudun Matawalle Community Development Association, Katsina, and as a member of the Tsauri Development Association, where they promoted peacebuilding, youth inclusion, and rural transformation. As part of his professional contribution to educational capacity building, he developed training manuals for primary and secondary school teachers under the AGILE and PLBC capacity-building programs, which have been widely used across the state and country. His guiding belief remains that education and service to humanity are the strongest tools for building a prosperous, self-reliant, and peaceful Katsina State.
